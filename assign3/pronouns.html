    • Pronouns

    Pronouns take the place of nouns. People do not always speak other people's names, so we shorten it by using pronouns. In a language like Spanish, the verbs change around the pronoun ("Voy al bano" vs "Vamanos al bano"). In English, the nouns are replaced. For example:

    "John is at work." vs "He is at work."

    "John is going to the store. I am going there, too."

    "I'm looking for the bathroom. Do you know where it is?"

    In these sentences, he, I/I'm, there, you, and it are all pronouns. He replaced John; there replaces store; and it replaces bathroom. The following are all pronouns:
