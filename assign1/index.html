    • Transmedia Storytelling

    Transmedia storytelling was a term coined by Henry Jenkins in 2003; it's using various forms of media (video, text, imagery, music, gaming, and more) together to tell a cohesive story or to expand on an established world to make it more complex and interesting.

    While primarily used in either entertainment or marketing, transmedia storytelling can also be used to successfully engage students in an educational setting. Transmedia storytelling is multimodal, hitting many of the learning styles of modern learners while incorporating technology in a most unique way.

    This website is meant to be used to help learn about transmedia storytelling and its various forms so that it can be understood and used properly in any setting, though primarily educational.
